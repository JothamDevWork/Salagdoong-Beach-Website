Culture – History, Traditions & Local Crafts

History of Siquijor & Salagdoong

An island steeped in mystery, faith, and natural heritage

Siquijor, known historically as "Isla del Fuego" (Island of Fire) by Spanish colonizers — referring to the bioluminescent glow seen from the sea — has long carried an air of mystery. The island gained its reputation for folk healing and herbal medicine, practices that continue to this day. Salagdoong Beach, nestled within a man-made molave forest in the municipality of Maria, reflects the island's tradition of caring for its natural environment. The forest was planted generations ago as both a source of timber and a buffer against coastal erosion.

Local Traditions & Beliefs

Siquijor's rich cultural heritage sets it apart from other Philippine islands

🌿 Folk Healing (Mananambal)

  • Traditional healers using herbal medicine
  • Potions brewed from local forest plants
  • Practiced during Holy Week in rituals
  • Respected throughout the Visayas region

🌳 The Old Enchanted Balete Tree

  • Over 400-year-old sacred balete tree in Lazi
  • Believed to be inhabited by spirits
  • One of Siquijor's top cultural landmarks
  • Small spring with fish for foot spa at base

⛪ Spanish Colonial Heritage

  • Lazi Convent — largest convent in Asia
  • St. Isidore de Labrador Church (1884)
  • Spanish-era cobblestone roads remain
  • Strong Catholic faith practiced island-wide

Local Crafts & Souvenirs

Take home a piece of Siquijor's culture

🧵 Woven Handicrafts

  • Banig (woven mats) from local artisans
  • Handmade baskets and bags from bamboo
  • Available at the Siquijor town market
  • Affordable prices — great pasalubong (gift)

🌿 Herbal Products

  • Locally made herbal oils and liniments
  • Sold at markets and roadside stalls
  • Ingredients sourced from Siquijor forests
  • Popular as wellness souvenirs

🐚 Shell Crafts

  • Seashell jewelry, necklaces, and keychains
  • Made by local community cooperatives
  • Sold near beach areas and market stalls
  • Good quality at budget-friendly prices

🍹 Local Beverages

  • Lambanog — traditional coconut wine
  • Basi — sugarcane wine from Visayas
  • Tuba — fresh coconut toddy
  • Widely available at local stores
