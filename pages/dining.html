Dining – Local Cuisine & Restaurants

What to Eat at Salagdoong

Fresh seafood and classic Filipino flavors await you

Grilled Seafood

  • Fresh fish grilled over charcoal
  • Served with steamed rice and calamansi
  • Popular at the beach resort restaurant
  • Approx. ₱150–₱350 per dish

Sinigang & Kare-kare

  • Classic Filipino soups and stews
  • Sinigang: sour tamarind broth with seafood
  • Kare-kare: peanut-based oxtail stew
  • Available at nearby eateries in Maria

Beachside Snacks

  • Cold drinks and buko (coconut) juice
  • Banana cue and camote cue (street food)
  • Chicharon (crispy pork rinds)
  • Fresh fruit slices from vendors

🍽️ Dining at the Beach

The on-site restaurant at Salagdoong serves rice meals (₱65–₱75), pasta dishes, and pizza (~₱150). Prices are slightly higher than local eateries. For better value, eat lunch at a nearby carenderia (local diner) in Maria before heading to the beach.

🎒 Pack Your Own Food

  • Picnic tables and shaded areas are available
  • Bringing your own packed lunch saves money
  • There are barbecue pits for rent at ₱50
  • Better option during peak season to avoid long queues
