Getting Here – Transport Options

Getting to Siquijor Island

Step-by-step guide to reaching the island from major Philippine cities

01

Fly to Dumaguete or Tagbilaran

The two nearest airports with frequent flights from Manila and Cebu. Dumaguete Airport (DGT) is the closest gateway.

  • Manila → Dumaguete (~1.5 hrs flight)
  • Cebu → Dumaguete (~30 min flight)
  • Manila → Tagbilaran (~1 hr flight)
02

Take the Ferry to Siquijor

Fast craft ferries run daily from Dumaguete and Tagbilaran ports to Siquijor town port.

  • Dumaguete → Siquijor (~45 min)
  • Tagbilaran → Siquijor (~2 hrs)
  • Oceanjet & Montenegro Lines operate routes
03

Travel to Salagdoong Beach

From Siquijor town port, Salagdoong Beach is on the eastern side (Maria). Multiple transport options available.

  • Tricycle hire: ~₱400–₱600 one-way
  • Scooter rental: ~₱400/day
  • About 20–30 km from the port

On-Island Transport Options

Getting around Siquijor once you've arrived

Transport Approx. Cost Best For
Scooter / Motorbike Rental ₱350–₱500/day Solo travelers, flexible itinerary
Tricycle Hire (whole day) ₱800–₱1,200/day Families, groups, no riding experience
Tricycle (one-way trip) ₱100–₱200 Short distances within town
Habal-habal (motorcycle taxi) ₱50–₱150 Budget-friendly, shorter trips
Jeepney (public) ₱15–₱40 Backpackers, very budget travel

🛵 Pro Tip: Rent a scooter right at the ferry terminal upon arrival. Riding around Siquijor is one of the most memorable experiences the island offers — just wear a helmet and drive carefully on narrow roads!
