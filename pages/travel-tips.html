Travel Tips – Your Visitor Guide

Best Time to Visit

Plan your trip around the right season for the best experience

Month Weather Crowds Recommendation
November – January Cool & dry Low ⭐ Best time — calm seas, clear water
February – May Hot & dry Moderate–High ✅ Great weather, peak tourist season
April (Holy Week) Very hot Very High ⚠️ Crowded — book early, go on weekdays
June – October Rainy / Typhoon Low ❌ Avoid — rough seas, activities may be closed

Safety & Practical Tips

Make the most of your visit with these helpful reminders

🏊 Swimming & Diving Safety

  • Only cliff dive at HIGH tide — always ask the lifeguard
  • Tides are deeper in the morning (8–11 AM ideal)
  • Don't dive if you cannot swim confidently
  • Be aware of rocks just below the water surface
  • Non-swimmers should stay in shallow areas

🎒 What to Bring

  • Sunscreen (reef-safe recommended)
  • Enough drinking water (limited vendors)
  • Cash — no ATMs at the beach itself
  • Snorkel gear (or rent on-site for ₱100)
  • Change of clothes and a towel
  • Waterproof bag or dry pouch for valuables

💰 Fees & Budget Guide

  • Entrance fee: ₱30–₱50 per person
  • Parking fee: ₱20–₱30 for motorcycles
  • Picnic shed rental: ₱50
  • Pool use (if available): ₱50 adults, ₱25 kids
  • Kayak rental: ₱100–₱200/hour
  • Snorkel rental: ₱100

🌿 Environmental Reminders

  • Do NOT litter — bring your trash out
  • Do not step on or damage coral reefs
  • Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine life
  • Respect the Salagdoong Forest — don't pick plants
  • Leave the beach cleaner than you found it

⏰ Operating Hours

  • Beach hours: 6:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily
  • Cliff platforms close at 5:00 PM sharp
  • Arrive early on weekends to avoid crowds
  • Best visit day: Weekday mornings
  • Peak hours: 10 AM – 2 PM on weekends

📱 Connectivity & Facilities

  • Limited mobile signal in some areas
  • No Wi-Fi at the beach
  • Restrooms available (cleanliness varies)
  • Changing rooms on-site
  • Nearest ATM: Siquijor town (~20 km away)

🌟 Top Tip: Visit Salagdoong on a weekday morning during the dry season (November–May). You'll have the platforms almost to yourself, the tides are at their best, and the water is crystal clear. Combine it with a stop at Cambugahay Falls and Lazi Convent for the perfect Siquijor day trip!
